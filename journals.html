Journaling Ideas

"Journaling is one of the most powerful tools we have to transform our lives." - Deepak Chopra
All of these journaling ideas can be done either writing on paper, typing it out on a computer, or even in a notes app on your phone!

Avoidance Journal For Anxiety

How: At the end of each day reflect on the situations that you felt you wanted to avoid because of anxiety. Write down or type out what the situation was, how much anxiety you were feeling and wether you pushed through the situation or avoided it. This journal is to help you be more aware of the avoidence tendencies that anxiety can cause. Remember: "avoidance feeds anxiety!"

Negative Thoughts Journal

How: Throughout the day, or at the end of the day notice what negative thoughts you've been having. Write them down, and then for each one write down the evidence for this thought and evidence against this thought. Then after that write down what a more realistic thought would be. Alot of times the negative thoughts we have are just "automatic negative thoughts" that aren't based in reality or how we really are.

What Went Well Today Journal

How: Reflect on the day and write down all of the things or situations that went well for you today, or that helped you feel positive emotions.

Free Writing Journal

How: Don't give this one too much thought, just open up a page, and write whatever comes to mind. If you've been stressed you may find that writing or typing it all out can really help relieve that stress, or if your mind has been feeling loud writing it all out can help you return to a sense of calm.

Dream Journal

How: The first step is before bed to set the intention: I'm going to write down whatever dreams I have tonight. Then throughout the night (if you wake up in the night) or in the morning write out whatever you remember of your dreams, no matter how small. As you continue this practice you may notice you start remembering more and more dreams, as well as the dreams becoming clearer and more vivid. This practice can also improve sleep quality.

Eating Symptom Journal

How: This journal is to help you to see paterns in how you feel after eating certain foods, to help you find what foods give you energy and make you feel good and which foods tend to bring you down. For example an entry could be: "May 27, 2024 Ate a whole pizza tonight, feeling super tired and have to go to bed early."
